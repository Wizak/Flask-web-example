Data Science

Data science uses techniques and theoretical developments from many fields of mathematics, statistics, operations research, scientific informatics and computer science, including signal processing, probabilistic models, machine learning, statistical learning, classification, clustering, data mining, databases data, pattern recognition theory, visualization, predictive analytics, fuzzy modeling, data warehouses, data compression, programming, artificial intelligence, etc. Methods designed for big data are a separate object of consideration in data science, but science itself is not limited to them; in addition, big data technologies often focus on organizing and pre-processing data rather than analyzing it. The development of machine learning has led to the growing importance of data science and its faster development. Data science influences research and applied research in many fields, including machine translation, speech recognition, robotics, search engines, the digital economy, and the biological sciences, computer technology in medicine, health care, and the humanities. It has a strong impact on the economy, business and finance. From a business perspective, data science is an integral part of the analysis of competitors - a new industry that covers areas such as data mining and data analysis.
